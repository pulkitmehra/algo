The Nobel Peace Prize (Swedish: Nobels fredspris) is one of the five Nobel Prizes created by the 
Swedish industrialist, inventor, and armaments manufacturer Alfred Nobel, along with the prizes in 
Chemistry, Physics, Physiology or Medicine, and Literature. Since March 1901,[3] it has been awarded annually (with some exceptions) to 
those who have "done the most or the best work for fraternity between nations, for the abolition or reduction of standing armies and for 
the holding and promotion of peace congresses
