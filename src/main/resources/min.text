London, Gandhi first employed nonviolent civil disobedience as an expatriate lawyer in South Africa, in the resident Indian community's 
struggle for civil rights. After his return to India in 1915, he set about organising peasants, farmers, and urban labourers to 
protest against excessive land-tax and discrimination. Assuming leadership of the Indian National Congress in 1921, Gandhi
